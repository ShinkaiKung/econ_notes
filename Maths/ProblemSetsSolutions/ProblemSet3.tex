\subsection*{Problem 1}

Solution:

a)

\begin{align*}
  ||f(x)||&=\sqrt{f \cdot f } = \sqrt{\int_{1}^{e} \sqrt{x}\sqrt{x}\ln x \, dx} \\
  &= \sqrt{\int_{1}^{e} x \ln x \, dx} \\
  &= \sqrt{\Big[\frac{1}{2}x ^{2}\ln x\Big]_{1}^{e} - \int_{1}^{e} \frac{x}{2}  \, dx} \\
  &= \sqrt{\frac{e ^{2}}{2}-\frac{e ^{2} - 1}{4}} \\
  &= \sqrt{\frac{1+e ^{2}}{4}} = \frac{\sqrt{1+ e ^{2}}}{2}
\end{align*}


b)

If $f$ and $g$ are orthogonal, we have $f \cdot g = 0$.

\begin{align*}
  f \cdot g &= \int_{1}^{e} c(a+bx)\ln x \, dx \\
  &= ac \int_{1}^{e} \ln x \, d + bc \int_{1}^{e} x \ln x \, dx \\
  &= ac \Big[x \ln x - x\Big]_{1}^{e} + bc \frac{1+e ^{2}}{4} \\
  &= ac + bc \frac{1+e ^{2}}{4} \\
  &= \frac{4ac + bc + bc e ^{2}}{4} \\
  &\Rightarrow 4ac + bc + bc e ^{2} = 0 \\
  &\Rightarrow 4a + b + b e ^{2} = 0 \\
  &a = - \frac{1+e ^{2}}{4}b
\end{align*}

A simple first order polynomial is $g(x) = -\frac{1+e ^{2}}{4} + x$.
